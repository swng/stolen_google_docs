ZyphDoz’s Double

It starts with a double, it breaks your b2b and it’s surprisingly good.

Bag 1

You can get a double 100% of the time with this build.

If J comes before L, do this version. If L comes before J, do the mirror.

Your field after this double is extremely versatile and transitions nicely into a strong midgame.
This field is actually so good it’s very viable to not continue the setup and just start your midgame from here.

There is also a 20% PC chance with T + L + S/Z.

Bag 2, 3 and 4

This has a 100% build chance and leaves you with a setup that has very good spiking potential.

With the right queue you can get a single->TSD->TSD->Quad. And when you’re not that lucky you can upstack until you get the 5th T piece and you will have a very high chance of getting two TSDs into a combo. This is actually really good because the messy garbage from your combo comes after 10 lines of clean garbage which makes it very hard for your opponent to block your messy garbage unless they have a big spike of their own.

Strategy

You should aim to accept your opponent's first attack and send your double right after, as getting garbage early is useful for deciding whether or not to go for the full setup.

Another benefit of sending your double after accepting their first attack is that they will either have to accept it, or cancel it with their second attack. A lot of openers tend to do a B2B TSD with their 2nd T-piece, so if they cancel your double it is very likely that they by doing so will send you 4 lines. In the best case scenario, if their first attack was also a TSD you will have 4-4 garbage to counterspike with.

If the opponent uses an opener that doesn’t send early such as MS2, you should send your double right away to stop them from doing a PC later on and also to make it harder for them to access the clean garbage and counterspike.

Just because it’s always possible to do the full setup doesn’t mean you should always do it.

These are my recommendations for when to start your midgame based on which column your first garbage hole is in.

Note that you can still do the full setup every time if you want to, it’s just that your boardstate after the initial double is so good I believe that always going for the full setup will on average put you in a worse position in four of these cases.
